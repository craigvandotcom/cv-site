## Newsletter Strategy for Craig

### Philosophy
The newsletter is the direct relationship with your audience. Unlike social media, you own the list and the connection. It's where trust compounds.

### Recommendation: Dual-Track Approach

#### Track 1: Weekly Insights (Short)
**Name options:**
- "Signal"
- "The Pulse"
- "Weekly Systems"

**Format:**
- 1 health insight from the week
- 1 quote that resonated
- 1 question to sit with
- Optional: 1 link to deeper content

**Length:** 300-500 words
**Frequency:** Weekly (consistent day)
**Purpose:** Stay top of mind, build ritual

---

#### Track 2: Deep Dives (Long)
**Name options:**
- "Essays"
- "Frameworks"
- Same newsletter, different type

**Format:**
- Long-form essay (1500-3000 words)
- Single topic, thoroughly explored
- Actionable framework or insight

**Frequency:** When ready (no forced schedule initially)
**Purpose:** Thought leadership, SEO, shareable content

---

### Combined Approach

**Option A: Single Newsletter, Variable Format**
- Weekly short update
- Monthly (or when ready) long-form essay
- Same list, same sender

**Option B: Two Separate Lists**
- "Signal" for weekly quick hits
- "Essays" for long-form (less frequent)
- Let subscribers choose

**Recommendation:** Start with Option A. Simpler to manage. Find cadence first, then potentially split later.

## Cadence Strategy

### Phase 1: Find Your Rhythm (First 3 months)
- Don't commit to a public schedule
- Send when you have something valuable
- Track what resonates
- Notice your natural creation rhythm

### Phase 2: Establish Pattern (Months 4-6)
- Identify sustainable frequency
- Make it public and consistent
- Weekly short + monthly long is common pattern

### Phase 3: Scale (6+ months)
- Consider splitting into tracks
- Add automation (YouTube → Newsletter derivative)
- Segment audience by interest

## Lead Magnet Options

### Option 1: The Framework PDF
"The Three Zones Protocol: A Quick-Start Guide"
- 5-10 page PDF
- Visual framework diagram
- Getting started checklist
- Links to app and deeper content

**Pros:** Immediate value, specific to Body Compass
**Cons:** Requires creation, may feel dated

---

### Option 2: Email Course
"7 Days to Body Literacy"
- Day 1: Why your body signals matter
- Day 2: The Three Zones explained
- Day 3: Setting up your tracking
- Day 4: Reading patterns
- Day 5: Common mistakes
- Day 6: The mindset shift
- Day 7: Next steps

**Pros:** Extended engagement, teaches while building relationship
**Cons:** More work to create, requires email automation

---

### Option 3: No Lead Magnet (Newsletter Value Only)
"Weekly health systems essays. No spam."
- The newsletter IS the value
- Simple, honest positioning
- Lower friction signup

**Pros:** Authentic, low maintenance, attracts right people
**Cons:** Lower conversion rate

---

### Recommendation
Start with **Option 3** (newsletter value only).

Once you have 10-20 essays published, create **Option 1** (framework PDF) as a compilation/summary. This happens naturally as you create content.

## Platform Recommendation

### Options Considered

| Platform | Pros | Cons |
|----------|------|------|
| **Substack** | Free, discovery features, simple | Limited customization, platform dependency |
| **ConvertKit** | Powerful automation, creator-focused | Cost as list grows |
| **Beehiiv** | Modern, good analytics, monetization | Newer platform |
| **Ghost** | Own your platform, members | Self-hosted complexity |

### Recommendation: Beehiiv or ConvertKit

**Beehiiv** if:
- You want growth features (referrals, recommendations)
- Modern analytics matter
- Potential future monetization

**ConvertKit** if:
- You want email courses/automation
- Integration with other tools matters
- Established, reliable platform

Both work well. **Start with Beehiiv** for the modern UX and growth features.
